Evolutionary Algorithm Fraud Detection:
Interpretable Rule Discovery

CS 420 Project Poster

Introduction & Motivation

  • Fraud operations need both strong detection and explainable decisions.
  • Black-box models can be accurate but difficult to audit and trust.
  • Goal: evolve human-readable fraud rules that prioritize likely fraud early.
  • Evaluated on Easy Financial, Hard Financial, and Insurance Fraud datasets.

Methods / Approach

  • Preprocess data and train ElasticNet as baseline risk scoring model.
  • Represent candidate fraud rules with threshold conditions on features.
  • EA loop: initialize population → fitness evaluation → diversity clustering.
  • Tournament selection + crossover + mutation across generations.
  • Fitness balances precision, recall, and agreement with baseline signal.

Conclusion

  • EA-discovered rules captured a large fraction of fraud in top-ranked investigations, often approaching 100% by top-10% to top-20% coverage.
  • Performance was strongest on easier and insurance-style datasets; hard financial data remains the key challenge.
  • Moderate mutation rates (roughly 0.1–0.3) were stable; overly high mutation reduced F1 consistency.
  • Main takeaway: interpretable evolved rules can achieve practical fraud triage performance while remaining auditable.
  • Future work: cost-sensitive objectives, richer rule structures, and drift-aware retraining.
